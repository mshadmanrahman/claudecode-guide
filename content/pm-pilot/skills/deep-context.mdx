## What it does

/deep-context runs a multi-source search across all your connected systems simultaneously. Jira tickets, Slack threads, Confluence pages, and your memory files. It synthesizes everything into a coherent summary of what's known about a topic.

The use case is speed. When you're handed a new problem or joining a project mid-stream, you need to understand what's already been decided, where the debates are, and what's still open. Doing that manually across four systems takes 45 minutes. /deep-context does it in 2.

## When to use it

- Onboarding to a new team or project where there's a lot of history
- Before a meeting on a topic you haven't been close to
- When someone asks "what's the status on X" and you don't know

## How to trigger it

Say: `tell me everything about [topic]`

Example: `tell me everything about the free text search feature`

## What you need

Best results with Jira, Slack, and Confluence all connected. Each source adds a different type of context: Jira has the formal record, Slack has the real debate, Confluence has the decisions that made it to documentation. Without integrations, paste the relevant content manually.
