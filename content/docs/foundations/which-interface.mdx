## Claude is no longer one thing

I've used twelve of Claude's surfaces in the last month. Some of them on purpose. A couple by accident. Here's the thing nobody tells you when you're coming from ChatGPT: the *surface* you pick matters more than which model is running underneath.

Claude.ai in a browser is not the same experience as Claude in Excel. Claude in Excel is not the same as Cowork. Cowork is not the same as Claude Code. Each one is tuned for a different job. Pick the wrong one and the tool feels dumb. Pick the right one and the work disappears.

This page is the map I wish someone had handed me three months ago. Not a ranking. Not a "best" anything. Just: *you want to do X, use Y*. Frankly speaking, that's the question most people are actually asking.

If you're a developer, skip down to the Code section. Everyone else, start at the top.

<SectionBreak number="01" kicker="Match the job" title="Pick the Claude that matches the job" />

Ten jobs. Ten answers. If you only read one section of this page, make it this one.

<JobBlock
  icon={MessageCircle}
  question="I want to chat or do quick research"
  pickLabel="Claude.ai in the browser"
>

This is the ChatGPT-equivalent experience. Free tier works. Upload files, use Artifacts for building shareable tools, use Projects to keep a workspace scoped to one topic. Mobile app if you're away from your desk.

Default here if you're not sure. It's also the surface you'll use most even after you've learned the others.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={FileSpreadsheet}
  question="I want to analyze or build a spreadsheet"
  pickLabel="Claude for Excel"
>

Installs as a Microsoft 365 add-in and sits in a side pane inside Excel itself. Formulas, pivots, financial models, "explain what this sheet is doing." It cites cells, so you can trace where a number came from.

One honest caveat: **it does NOT require Microsoft 365 Copilot.** You just need a paid Claude plan (Pro or higher) and a compatible Office version. A lot of enterprise buyers get that wrong and scope a Copilot license they don't need.

Also: Claude for Excel doesn't save your chat history, and it wipes data after 30 days. If you work in finance, legal, or anything regulated, know that before you open a sensitive model.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Presentation}
  question="I want to build a slide deck"
  pickLabel="Claude for PowerPoint"
>

Same idea as Excel. Installs as an add-in. Reads your template, respects brand colors, generates and edits slides in place. A messy outline turns into a first-draft deck in a few minutes.

It's not perfect at brand compliance. Expect to clean up spacing and occasional font issues. But the structural work, layout, headline writing, turning bullets into prose, is genuinely good.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={FileText}
  question="I want to write or review a long document"
  pickLabel="Claude for Word"
>

Newest of the Office add-ins. Launched April 13. Lives in a sidebar, supports redlining, tracked changes, comments. Long-document review is where it shines. Drop in a 40-page spec, ask for inconsistencies across sections, and it surfaces things you would have missed on your third read.

Least mature of the three Office add-ins because it's the newest. A few rough edges. Still worth it if you live in Word.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Globe}
  question="I want Claude to help me while I browse the web"
  pickLabel="Claude for Chrome"
>

Browser extension. Opens as a side panel. Reads the page you're on, summarizes articles, runs scheduled tasks, moves across tabs. Think of it as "ChatGPT but it can see what you see."

Heads up: **it's in beta and requires a paid plan.** Not on the free tier.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={FolderSearch}
  question="I want to search my work email, Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint"
  pickLabel="Microsoft 365 Connector"
>

Most people miss this one, including me until last month. Different from the Office add-ins. The Connector lets Claude *read* across your Microsoft work stack. You ask "what did Ana commit to in her last three messages," and it surfaces the answer from Teams plus the relevant Outlook thread.

Two gotchas: **read-only** (Claude cannot send email or create calendar events through it), and **work tenants only** (personal `@outlook.com` accounts aren't supported). The Connector is available on all plans, including Free.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Workflow}
  question="I want Claude to run a multi-step task on its own"
  pickLabel="Cowork"
>

This is the one that breaks people's brains. Cowork is Claude with computer-use, connectors (Gmail, Drive, DocuSign, FactSet), and the ability to keep going across a long task without you babysitting it.

The honest version: Cowork is released as a product, but the *computer-use* piece, where Claude actually drives your desktop, is still research preview. Pro and Max plans only. Don't describe Cowork as "fully autonomous" to your colleagues yet. It's closer to "autonomous with training wheels."

I use Cowork most weeks for unglamorous work. Running lead-performance analysis against a 24-month cost-per-lead (CPL) export. Cross-referencing an ad-delivery report with a GA4 query. Cleaning up my Mac when the browser and memory are out of hand and I can't figure out which tab is eating RAM. Nothing flashy. A patient intern with a longer attention span than I have.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Smartphone}
  question="I want to hand a task off from my phone to my desktop"
  pickLabel="Cowork Dispatch"
>

You're on your phone, you start something ("research these five vendors, draft a comparison doc"), and when you get back to your laptop the work is done and waiting. It persists across devices. Ethan Mollick wrote about this and called it "the first truly useful agent handoff I've used." He's not wrong.

Research preview. Pro and Max only. Launched March 17. Small feature, big quality-of-life impact.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Terminal}
  question="I want to build software or write code"
  pickLabel="Claude Code"
>

This is the surface this guide was originally written for. If you're a developer, you already know. If you're not: Claude Code is a command-line tool (and increasingly a desktop app) that reads your entire repo, writes code, runs tests, debugs, and commits. It's the power-user surface.

Start with the [CLI](/docs/foundations/installation) if you're comfortable in a terminal, or the [web version](https://claude.ai/code) if you want to try it without installing anything. If you live in VS Code or JetBrains, the IDE extension is probably what you want.

</JobBlock>

<JobBlock
  icon={Package}
  question="I want to build my own product with Claude inside"
  pickLabel="Claude API + Agent SDK"

<SectionBreak number="03" kicker="Coming from ChatGPT" title="Three mental shifts that make this easier" />

Most people reading this page are ChatGPT users poking at Claude. That's who I was a year ago. Three things tripped me up more than anything else.

<MentalShiftsGrid>

<MentalShift number="1" headline="You don't pick a Claude, you pick a surface">

ChatGPT is one thing with variations. Web app, mobile app, API. Claude is twelve things, each tuned for a different job. Don't look for "the" Claude. Ask "which one for this task."

This took me two weeks to internalize. Once it clicked, every frustration melted. The tool wasn't bad. I was using it for the wrong job.

</MentalShift>

<MentalShift number="2" headline="Memory works differently">

Claude Memory became available on the free tier on March 2. Unlike ChatGPT's Memory, which remembers facts about you across everything, Claude's Memory stays inside Claude.ai chats and does *not* cross into Projects. Projects are deliberately siloed.

If you build Custom GPTs in ChatGPT, Projects is the closest equivalent. The architecture is different though: Projects are scoped workspaces with their own context, not custom-instruction wrappers.

</MentalShift>

<MentalShift number="3" headline="The import tool actually works">

Anthropic launched `claude.com/import-memory` a few weeks ago. You export your ChatGPT memory, import it into Claude, it carries over. I tried it. Worked better than expected. If you're switching, do this first. Don't start from zero.

</MentalShift>

<MentalShift number="4" headline="Treat Claude like a new intern, not a magic box">

Every surface gets better when you onboard Claude. Spend ten minutes doing what you'd do with a new hire: tell it who you are, what you're working on, and when to ask versus when to just do.

In Claude Code and Cowork that contract lives in a file called [`CLAUDE.md`](/docs/foundations/claude-md). In Claude.ai Projects it's custom instructions. Same idea. You're writing a contract with your intern, and the clearer the contract, the better the work.

The three clauses I always include: what I'm working on, how I prefer to work, and when to ask before doing anything destructive. Ten minutes up front saves hours mid-session. If you juggle work and personal projects, read [Keep Your Two Lives Apart](/docs/foundations/separate-contexts) before the two contracts get tangled.

</MentalShift>

</MentalShiftsGrid>

<SectionBreak number="06" kicker="FAQ" title="Questions I get asked a lot" />

### If I only buy one plan, which one?

Pro ($17/mo annual) covers every surface most people will touch: Excel, PowerPoint, Word, Cowork (with computer-use in preview), Chrome, Claude.ai, mobile, the Microsoft 365 Connector, and Claude Code. Max is worth it if you're running Claude Code daily or using Cowork for long agentic tasks, because the usage caps on Pro will bite. Team and Enterprise are about seats and admin, not features.

### Does Claude in Excel work better than Microsoft Copilot?

Different strengths. For formula generation and explaining existing sheets, Claude is sharper and cites cells better. For "turn this table into a chart," Microsoft Copilot is still a bit smoother. Most finance folks I've talked to run both side by side and use whichever answers faster. You're allowed to do that.

### Can I use Cowork on my phone?

Not for desktop control. The Dispatch handoff lets you *start* a Cowork task from mobile and come back to it on desktop, but the actual computer-use piece runs on your Mac or Windows machine. Phones don't host Cowork itself.

### What's the difference between Cowork and Claude Code?

Cowork is general-purpose agent work: research, document drafting, filling spreadsheets, driving your computer. Claude Code is specifically for software engineering: reading code repos, writing and running code, debugging. Same parent model, very different tuning. If you're not a developer, you don't need Claude Code. If you are a developer, Cowork and Claude Code handle different parts of your day.

### Is there a Claude for Outlook?

No standalone Outlook add-in exists. Outlook access ships through the Microsoft 365 Connector, which is read-only. You can ask Claude to find or summarize emails; it can't send them.

### What about Gemini or ChatGPT for this kind of thing?

I use Claude for work and personal knowledge. I use ChatGPT for image generation sometimes. Gemini I dropped a while ago. But the real answer: the surface map matters more than the brand. The tools that win are the ones tuned for a specific job. If Gemini adds a sharper PowerPoint flow tomorrow, I'd try it. Tools rotate. The work doesn't.

### How do I stay current with new surfaces?

Anthropic ships fast. Three new surfaces shipped in Q1 alone. I update this page monthly. If you subscribe to the newsletter below, you'll get updates when they land. That's the most reliable signal I know.
