## Who this is for

Content marketers, growth marketers, brand managers, and anyone who publishes: this page is for you. If you manage a content calendar, run campaigns, or write copy, Claude can do the heavy drafting while you handle the strategy.

## The mindset shift

You know your audience better than any AI. Claude does not. What it has is pattern: it knows how to structure a campaign brief, write a cold email sequence, repurpose a long-form post into five social variations, and analyze why a subject line might underperform. Your job is to give it your audience knowledge and brand voice. Its job is to produce at scale while you direct.

That split is where the time savings come from. You stop being the person typing the first draft. You become the editor, the strategist, the person with taste. Claude handles the blank page. You decide what stays.

The one thing that trips most marketers up early on: they give Claude too little context and then wonder why the output sounds generic. Generic input, generic output. When you feed it your personas, your tone guide, your past top performers, and your campaign goal, the first draft actually sounds like you.

## Your 5C Loop as a marketer

The [5C Loop](/docs/frameworks/5c-loop) is the core framework this site teaches. Here is what each step looks like for marketing work.

**Capture:** Brand voice notes, campaign goals, audience persona, competitor examples, and any raw research or data you have. Dump it in before you ask for anything. The more you bring, the less you fix later.

**Context:** Current campaigns, existing content library, what has performed well, the channel you are targeting, and any constraints around legal review, tone, or length. Claude writes to what you give it. Channel context alone changes the output significantly.

**Create:** Campaign briefs, content drafts, email sequences, social variations, SEO outlines, ad copy. This is where Claude earns its keep. Let it produce a full first draft before you start editing.

**Check:** Brand alignment, tone consistency, compliance requirements, and whether the call to action is clear and right for the audience. This is your pass, not Claude's. You know when something sounds off. Trust that.

**Compound:** Save your best prompts as templates. Build a prompt library for every content type you repeat monthly, quarterly, or per campaign. The second time you run a workflow, it should be faster than the first.

## Four workflows worth learning first

### Write a campaign brief from a goal

Give Claude the campaign goal, audience, channel, and any constraints. It produces a structured brief with positioning, messaging pillars, and success metrics. This is especially useful before handing work to a contractor or kicking off an internal sprint, because everyone is working from the same document instead of a scattered Slack thread.

### Repurpose long-form content across channels

Paste your blog post, webinar transcript, or interview. Ask Claude for a LinkedIn post, three tweet variations, and an email summary. Each output starts from your words and your thinking, which means the ideas stay yours even as the format changes.

### Generate A/B variations for email or ads

Give Claude the original copy and tell it what variable you want to test. Subject line, CTA, opening sentence, tone. It produces variations with clear hypotheses about what each one optimizes for, so you are making an informed choice instead of guessing.

### Analyze content performance and suggest adjustments

Paste your performance data with context about the content. Claude helps you identify patterns, surface hypotheses about why something underperformed, and suggest specific adjustments for the next iteration. You still decide what to act on. But having a thinking partner that does not need background catch-up saves a lot of time.

## Your starter CLAUDE.md

A `CLAUDE.md` file is a plain text briefing document that lives in your project folder. It tells Claude who you are, what your context is, and how to help you. You write it once and update it as things change.

```markdown
# CLAUDE.md

## About me
I'm a marketer. I create content, run campaigns, and manage brand voice. I don't write code.

## My context
- Brand: [description]
- Target audience: [description]
- Channels I manage: [list]
- Brand voice: [2-3 adjectives or a brief description]

## How to help me
- Campaign briefs from raw goals
- Content drafts in our brand voice
- Repurposing content across channels
- Email and ad copy variations for A/B testing
- Performance analysis and iteration suggestions

## What matters
- Always match the brand voice described above
- Shorter is better for most copy
- Suggest alternatives when something feels off
- Flag when a claim needs a source
```
