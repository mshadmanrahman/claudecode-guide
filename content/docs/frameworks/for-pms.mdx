## Who this is for

This page is for product managers, product owners, and APMs who spend more time wrangling documents than thinking about the product. If your job is turning ambiguity into clarity, Claude handles the next step: turning your clarity into documents.

## The mindset shift

Good PMs think clearly. That is the hard part, and you already do it. The bottleneck is rarely the thinking itself. It is the translation layer: turning a messy conversation into a PRD, a sprint of research into a one-pager, a stakeholder email thread into an exec update.

Claude is fluent in that translation. You give it the thinking. It gives you the first draft. You make it yours.

The shift is small but it changes how your afternoons feel. Instead of opening a blank Notion page and staring at it, you start with something to react to. Editing is faster than writing from scratch. Your opinions sharpen when you have something to push against. That is the loop Claude fits into.

## Your 5C Loop as a PM

The [5C Loop](/docs/frameworks/5c-loop) is the core framework this site teaches. Here is what each step looks like when your deliverable is a doc, not a design.

**Capture:** Dump in everything raw. Slack messages, interview notes, customer feedback tickets, that Notion page you half-wrote at 11pm. Unpolished is fine. Incomplete is fine. Claude needs your raw material, not your finished thinking.

**Context:** Tell Claude where you are in the roadmap, what the product strategy is, who the users are, and what a good outcome looks like for this quarter. A sentence or two per item is enough. The more specific you are here, the less editing you do in the next step.

**Create:** PRDs, user stories, discovery briefs, exec summaries, weekly updates, OKR check-ins. Let Claude produce a full first draft before you start editing. Your job at this stage is direction, not typing.

**Check:** Ask Claude to identify what is missing, surface unstated assumptions, or flag where the logic breaks down. Treat it like a sharp colleague who has read everything and has no agenda. They will tell you what the doc is not saying.

**Compound:** Every doc you produce becomes a template. The next PRD starts from the last one. The best prompts get saved. Over a quarter, you build a library of starting points that get faster and better every time.

## Four workflows worth learning first

### Write a PRD from rough notes

Paste your rough notes, decisions made, and open questions into Claude alongside your PRD template. Claude produces a first draft. You spend your time editing and sharpening, not staring at a blank page. Key pattern: include what the feature is trying to accomplish, what problem it solves for users, and what success looks like numerically. The more you front-load the strategy, the less you have to fix in revision.

### Synthesize user research into findings

Paste interview transcripts or survey responses and ask Claude for themes, a direct quote per theme, and a one-sentence insight per theme that a stakeholder could act on. This is especially useful before a product review or when writing a discovery brief. What used to take half a day of affinity mapping now takes twenty minutes of prompt-and-edit.

### Prepare for a roadmap review

Give Claude your current roadmap, the stakeholder priorities you are walking into, and the questions you expect to face. Claude helps you stress-test your reasoning and anticipates the hard questions before you walk in the room. No more getting caught flat-footed on a tradeoff you had not articulated out loud.

### Write a weekly update or exec summary

Paste your sprint notes or the week's Slack threads. Claude writes a clear, concise update with a one-line situation, key progress, blockers, and next steps. You review and send. The whole thing takes five minutes instead of forty-five.

## Your starter CLAUDE.md

A `CLAUDE.md` file sits in your project folder and tells Claude who you are and how to help. Copy this, fill in the brackets, and drop it in your project root. You will never have to re-explain your context again.

```markdown
# CLAUDE.md

## About me
I'm a product manager. I write docs, run discovery, and align stakeholders. I don't write code.

## My context
- Product area: [description]
- Current quarter goals: [description]
- Key stakeholders: [list]
- Team setup: [description]

## How to help me
- First drafts of PRDs, discovery briefs, and user stories
- Synthesizing user research and interview notes
- Preparing for roadmap reviews and stakeholder meetings
- Turning messy notes into clean exec summaries

## Format preferences
- Short paragraphs, not walls of text
- Numbered lists for steps, bullet points for options
- Flag assumptions and missing information clearly
```
