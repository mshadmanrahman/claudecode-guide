## Who this is for

This page is for K-12 teachers, university educators, and corporate trainers: the people who shape what others understand. If you spend hours on lesson plans, assessments, and parent communication, Claude can give most of those hours back.

## The mindset shift

Every lesson plan you write follows a pattern you already know. Learning objectives, activities, assessment, differentiation. Claude knows that pattern too. Give it your context and it produces a first draft in seconds. You spend your energy on what only you can do: adjusting for the specific kid in front of you, the mood in the room, the thing that did not land last Tuesday.

Claude handles the scaffolding so you can do the teaching. Think of it like a very organized teaching assistant who never complains about grading, has infinite patience for generating rubric variations, and will never forget to include a formative check at the end of the lesson.

The one adjustment worth making: front-load your context. Grade level, subject, class size, the standard you are targeting. The more precisely you set the scene, the less you have to rewrite in the draft you get back.

## Your 5C Loop as a teacher

The [5C Loop](/docs/frameworks/5c-loop) is the core framework this site teaches. Here is what each step looks like in your classroom.

**Capture:** Start with your learning objective, the topic, and any constraints. What do students need to know or be able to do by the end of this lesson? Even a rough note is enough to get started.

**Context:** Add the details that shape the output. Grade level, subject, class size, any specific student needs, the standards you are aligning to, and what you tried last time. This is the step most people skip, and it is also the step that makes the draft actually usable.

**Create:** Lesson plans, quiz questions, rubrics, differentiated activities, parent newsletters, student feedback templates. Claude produces a full first draft, and your job shifts from writing to editing and adjusting for your actual students.

**Check:** Ask Claude to review for age-appropriateness, alignment to your stated objectives, or differentiation for different learning levels. It is a fast second read before anything goes in front of students or parents.

**Compound:** Save the lesson plans that worked. Build a library of assessment templates for your subject. Reuse and adapt instead of starting from scratch every unit. Your future self will feel the difference.

## Four workflows worth learning first

### Write a lesson plan from a learning objective

Give Claude the objective, grade level, time available, and any resources you are using. It produces a structured plan with activities, timing, and a formative check built in. You adjust for your class, your room, and the three students who will definitely need a different entry point.

### Generate quiz or discussion questions

Paste the topic or a reading excerpt and ask for questions at different Bloom's taxonomy levels. Useful for differentiation, formative checks, or when you need a discussion starter for a class that has gone quieter than expected.

### Create a rubric for an assignment

Describe the assignment and what excellent work looks like. Claude produces a rubric with criteria and performance levels you can edit in seconds, rather than building one from a blank table at 10pm the night before it is due.

### Write personalized student feedback

Share the assignment brief and a short description of the student's work. Claude drafts specific, constructive feedback that you can adjust to your own voice before it reaches the student or their family.

## Your starter CLAUDE.md

Copy this into your project and fill in the brackets. This is the context file Claude reads to understand your teaching situation before every conversation.

```markdown
# CLAUDE.md

## About me
I'm a teacher. I work with [grade level] students in [subject]. My goal is to save time on planning and admin so I can focus on students.

## My context
- School level: [K-12 / Higher Ed / Corporate Training]
- Subject: [description]
- Class size: [number]
- Standards: [e.g., Common Core, IB, local curriculum]

## How to help me
- Lesson plans with clear objectives, activities, and timing
- Quiz and discussion questions at different complexity levels
- Rubrics and assessment criteria
- Parent and guardian communication
- Differentiated activity variations

## Tone preferences
- Professional but warm
- Avoid jargon students wouldn't understand
- Keep instructions clear and sequential
```
