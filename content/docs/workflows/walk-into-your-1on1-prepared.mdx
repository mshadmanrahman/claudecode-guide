## The prompt

Drop this into Claude with the person's name in the placeholder. Run it the morning of the meeting, not the day before.

<PromptCard
  id="walk-1on1-primary"
  variant="primary"
  prompt={`Prep me for my 1:1 with [name].

Pull recent emails between us, the last 4 meeting notes I have on them, and any open project items where they're a stakeholder.

Write a one-page brief with: Recent context (what's been happening with them), Open items (anything I owe them or they owe me), 3 questions I should ask them today, and 2 things I should be ready to answer.

Keep it skimmable. I'm walking to the meeting in 5 minutes.`}
/>

## What you get back

<FromClaude>

**1:1 brief: Sarah Chen** · Friday 9:00 AM

### Recent context
- Sarah took over the dashboard project from Marcus three weeks ago.
- Last sync (April 22): she flagged a Q3 headcount question for the Search team.
- Two stakeholder threads since then mention scope creep on the data platform.

### Open items
- You owe her: a one-pager on the Match Maker roadmap (mentioned April 22)
- She owes you: clarity on whether the dashboard handover scope is final
- Both: the six-month roadmap Sarah asked for "early May"

### Questions to ask
1. Is the Q3 headcount conversation moving with leadership yet?
2. What's the ceiling on what I can commit to in the dashboard handover before checking with you?
3. Are you OK with me front-loading discovery work in the new scope, or should delivery commitments come first?

### Be ready to answer
- "What does the new scope mean for the Match Maker roadmap?"
- "How are you thinking about Q3 OKRs given the recent reorg?"

</FromClaude>

## Variations

Same shape, different relationships.

<PromptCard
  id="walk-1on1-new-person"
  variant="variation"
  kicker="For a first 1:1 with someone new"
  prompt={`Prep me for my first 1:1 with [name]. I don't have any meeting history with them.

Pull what you can find: any emails, any Slack or Teams threads, any documents they've authored or commented on.

Help me build a profile: what they care about based on what they've written, who they collaborate with, where they sit in the org. Then suggest 5 open-ended questions I can use to get to know them.`}
/>

<PromptCard
  id="walk-1on1-quarterly"
  variant="variation"
  kicker="For a quarterly check-in"
  prompt={`Prep me for my quarterly check-in with [name]. It's been about 12 weeks since we last spoke.

Pull our last 3 conversations and surface: what was outstanding then, what's changed in their org since, and what I committed to that I haven't followed up on.

Don't sugarcoat the followups I missed. I'd rather walk in honest than blindsided.`}
/>

<PromptCard
  id="walk-1on1-skip-level"
  variant="variation"
  kicker="For a skip-level meeting"
  prompt={`Prep me for a skip-level with [name] (my manager's manager).

Pull what you can on their priorities right now: recent emails or comms they've sent broadly, any docs they own, the projects they've been mentioned on.

Then help me figure out: what does this person care about that's different from what my direct manager cares about, and what's worth raising with them that I haven't gotten traction on at the level below.`}
/>

## Tips and gotchas

**Run it the morning of, not the day before.** State changes overnight. That email Sarah sent at 11pm or that PR Marcus merged at 7am won't be in yesterday's snapshot.

**Don't paste secrets into the prompt.** Names, project names, meeting dates are fine. API keys, customer data, compensation numbers stay out.

**If the person doesn't show up in the brief, give Claude a hint.** "Sarah Chen, my manager since the reorg in March." One sentence of context fixes most cases where Claude couldn't find them.
